The problem

Waste is a lagging indicator.

$35B
lost annually in U.S. cold chain to preventable spoilage, most of it discovered only after the product is already gone.

By the time a pallet of strawberries is pulled from the floor, the loss has already happened. Your team has written it off, your customer has been short-shipped, and no one can explain which link in the chain actually failed.

Temperature loggers tell you what happened. Inventory systems tell you what you have. Nothing tells you what's about to go wrong, at which pallet, with what time to act.

Cryoline closes that gap. We ingest your sensor streams, warehouse data, and transit telemetry, score every pallet against its commodity's spoilage curve, and surface the ones that need a decision today, not next Monday.

How it works

Sensor in.
Decision out.

Four stages, running continuously. Data flows in from your existing infrastructure. Actionable recommendations flow out to the people who can take them.

01 / INGEST

Sensors and systems

Temperature, humidity, door events, transit GPS, WMS inventory. We connect to what you already run, no hardware replacement.

02 / MODEL

Spoilage scoring

Each pallet scored against its commodity's degradation curve. Our models are trained on commodity-specific shelf-life research plus your historical loss data.

03 / FLAG

Risk queue

Pallets sorted by time-to-spoil and value at risk. Your team wakes up to a ranked list, not a dashboard they have to interpret.

04 / ACT

Recommended action

Reroute, discount, divert, or dispose. Each recommendation comes with its expected recovery value, so your team knows why.

Four actions, one decision

Not "there's a problem."
"Here's what to do."

Most monitoring tools stop at the alert. Cryoline runs the math on every recovery path and recommends the one with the highest expected value, factoring in remaining shelf life, transit options, and your downstream customer network.

Reroute

Move it closer, fast.

When a pallet's shelf life won't survive the original route, we find a DC or customer within its remaining window and suggest the swap.

Example: Salmon → rerouted Oakland to Sacramento DC. Recovered $8,940.
Discount

Move it on price.

Generate a markdown plan for retail partners before the product visibly turns. Catches value that would otherwise go to dump.

Example: Strawberries → 40% off, moved same-day. Recovered $3,240.
Divert

Send it downstream.

Products that won't survive retail can still work for processors, food service, or secondary markets. We route to partners in your network.

Example: Ground beef → processor contract. Recovered $5,820 of $7,200.
Dispose

Cut losses cleanly.

When recovery math doesn't work, dispose fast and log it. Compliance-ready records for insurance claims and waste reporting.

Example: Mixed greens → documented disposal. Full audit trail retained.
